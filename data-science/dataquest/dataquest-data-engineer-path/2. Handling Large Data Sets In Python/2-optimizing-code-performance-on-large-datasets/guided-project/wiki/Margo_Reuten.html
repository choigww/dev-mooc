Margo Reuten (Maasbracht, abt. 1966) is a Dutch head chef, known for her cooking in the Michelin starred restaurant Da Vinci. Since 2009, she is the only female head chef holding two Michelin stars.[1]

Reuten describes her cooking style as "classic French" and "loyal to the region". For her classic dishes she uses as much as possible locally sourced ingredients.[2]

Career[edit]

Restaurant Da Vinci was opened in 1993. It carries one Michelin star since 1999 and two since 2009.[3][4][5][6][7][8]

Before Reuten opened restaurant Da Vinci, she had already a lot of experience on high culinary level. She worked as sous chef in restaurant Der Bloasbalg, at that moment bestowed with a Michelin star. Other restaurants where she worked and trained were Toine Hermsen (sous chef), De Swaen (chef de partie) and Prinses Juliana (apprentice chef).[9]

Persoonlijk[edit]

Reuten is raised in Maasbracht and married to Petro Kools.[10][11]

Awards[edit]

  • SVH Meesterkok (Master chef): 1990[12]
  • Michelin star Da Vinci: 1 star, 1999-2008; 2 stars: 2009-present
  • Freeman of Maasgouw: 2009[13]
  • GaultMillau Chef of the Year: 2012[14]
  • Relais & Chateaux Grand Chef: 2012[15]

Published books[edit]

  • 2010: Proeven van Bekwaamheid Special Edition (Eng.: Tasting Ability Special Edition)
