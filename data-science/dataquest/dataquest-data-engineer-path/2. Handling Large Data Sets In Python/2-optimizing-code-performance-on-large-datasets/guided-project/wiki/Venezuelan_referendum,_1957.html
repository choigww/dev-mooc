A referendum on the presidential term and national and regional governors was held in Venezuela on 15 December 1957.[1] Voters were asked whether they approved of President Marcos Pérez Jiménez remaining in power without fresh elections, and appointing government nominees as members of the national parliament, regional assemblies and local councils.[2] With the referendum held under non-democratic conditions,[2] the measures were reportedly approved by 86.7% of voters.

Results[edit]

Choice Votes %
For 2,374,790 86.7
Against 364,182 13.3
Invalid/blank votes 186,013 –
Total 2,924,985 100
