Justus Ferdinand Poggenburg II

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation, search

Justus Ferdinand Poggenburg II (December 10, 1865 – January 1917) was a New York City billiards champion. He was known as the "father of amateur billiards".[1] The Poggenburg Memorial Billiards Trophy Cup awarded by the National Association of Amateur Billiard Players is named in his honor.[2]

Biography[edit]

He was born on December 10, 1865, to Justus Ferdinand Poggenburg I (1840–1893), the botanist. He was the father of Justus Ferdinand Poggenburg III.[1][3] He died in January 1917.
