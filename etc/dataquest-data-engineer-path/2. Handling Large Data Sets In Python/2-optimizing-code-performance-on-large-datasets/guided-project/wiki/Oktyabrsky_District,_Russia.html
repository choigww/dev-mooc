Oktyabrsky District, Russia

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Oktyabrsky District is the name of several administrative and municipal divisions in Russia. The districts are generally named for the October Revolution of 1917.

Districts of the federal subjects[edit]

Federal subjects of Russia which have an entity called Oktyabrsky District
  • Oktyabrsky District, Amur Oblast, an administrative[1] and municipal[2] district of Amur Oblast
  • Oktyabrsky District, Chelyabinsk Oblast, an administrative and municipal district of Chelyabinsk Oblast[3]
  • Oktyabrsky District, Jewish Autonomous Oblast, an administrative[4] and municipal[5] district of the Jewish Autonomous Oblast
  • Oktyabrsky District, Republic of Kalmykia, an administrative[6] and municipal[7] district of the Republic of Kalmykia
  • Oktyabrsky District, Khanty–Mansi Autonomous Okrug, an administrative[8] and municipal[9] district of Khanty–Mansi Autonomous Okrug
  • Oktyabrsky District, Kostroma Oblast, an administrative[10] and municipal[11] district of Kostroma Oblast
  • Oktyabrsky District, Kursk Oblast, an administrative[12] and municipal[13] district of Kursk Oblast
  • Oktyabrsky District, Orenburg Oblast, an administrative[14] and municipal[15] district of Orenburg Oblast
  • Oktyabrsky District, Perm Krai, an administrative[16] and municipal[17] district of Perm Krai

  • Oktyabrsky District, Volgograd Oblast, an administrative[22] and municipal[23] district of Volgograd Oblast

Renamed districts[edit]

  • Oktyabrsky District, name of Takhtamukaysky District of the Republic of Adygea, Russia, until 1990
  • Oktyabrsky District, name of Nurlatsky District of the Republic of Tatarstan, Russia, until 1997

Historical districts[edit]

  • Oktyabrsky District, Kalinin Oblast, a former district in Leningrad, Western, Kalinin, and Velikiye Luki Oblasts, merged into Zapadnodvinsky District in 1963.[51]
  • Oktyabrsky District, Saint Petersburg, a district of the federal city of St. Petersburg; merged into the newly established Admiralteysky District in March 1994

Historical city districts[edit]

  • Oktyabrsky City District, a city district of Kaliningrad, the administrative center of Kaliningrad Oblast; merged into Tsentralny Administrative District in 2009[52]
